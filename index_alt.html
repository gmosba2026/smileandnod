The Smile & Nod Manifesto
i. Chapter One

Every card we make begins with a single, specific feeling.

There is a category of moment that doesn't quite fit inside a text message. A friend who held you up through something dark. A parent, finally retiring. The morning after the baby came home. These are not events, exactly. They are the emotional residue that events leave behind — and they deserve, we think, something you can put a hand on.

Smile & Nod was built around that conviction. Not a shop, not a factory, but a small atelier — two paper cutters, three calligraphers, one foil press, and a stack of cotton stock imported from a mill that has been making paper the same way since 1922.

We start every card with a conversation. Not a form. Not a dropdown. A conversation about who the person is, what the moment was, and what the sender wants them to feel when the envelope opens. From there, everything is composed around that feeling — the words, the typeface, the weight of the paper, the color of the foil, whether there's a ribbon or a wax seal or a small framed print tucked inside the box.

The cards we make cost more than greeting cards because they are not greeting cards. They are small, intentional objects, built to outlive the moment that prompted them. A shelf-kept thing. A shoebox-treasured thing. A thing someone's grown child will find, thirty years from now, and understand something about who loved whom.

That is, we think, a worthwhile thing to make.

In Conversation

A few questions, answered plainly.

Q · Why does a card cost what it costs?

— Because it is not, strictly speaking, a card. It is a small object made by several pairs of hands, written around a specific person, printed on paper that will outlast the moment. We don't price for volume; we price for care.

Q · Can I write the words myself?

— Most of our clients do, and we'd encourage it. We'll print your words exactly as written, or gently edit with you for rhythm. The point is that the words are yours.

Q · What if I can't find the right words?

— Then we help. Our concierge and our writing room exist for exactly that reason. Tell us who it's for. We'll offer you three drafts, and you'll pick the one that sounds most like you.

Q · How long does it take?

— Five to seven days for The Letter. Ten to fourteen for The Keepsake and The Archive, because the hand-finishing takes time. Rush is available; ask.

Q · Do you do corporate orders?

— Yes — for teams and studios that want to mark something without it feeling like a corporate gesture. Volume pricing begins at twenty-five cards.
